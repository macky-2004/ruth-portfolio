1.2 Voice Attributes
Table
Attribute	How It Sounds	Example
Client-Focused	Every sentence starts with "You" or implies the reader's benefit	"Your website should work as hard as you do"
Problem-Aware	Acknowledges real struggles before offering solutions	"Tired of websites that look good but don't bring in customers?"
Confidently Humble	Shows expertise without arrogance; lets results speak	"Here's how I helped a local boutique double their online inquiries"
Action-Oriented	Uses active verbs, creates momentum	"Let's build something that grows your business"
Warm & Human	Professional but never robotic; personality shines through	"When I'm not coding, you'll find me in my garden or creating mehendi art"
1.3 Words to Use vs. Words to Avoid
Table
Use These	Avoid These
Build, create, design, solve, grow, transform	Know, learned, studied, completed (too academic)
Your business, your customers, your goals	I, me, my (limit to 20% of sentences)
Fast, responsive, optimized, conversion-focused	HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript (only in technical context)
Results, outcomes, impact, growth	Features, functions, capabilities
Let's, together, we, your project	Alone, by myself, solo

plain

Why this works: Invites the visitor to self-identify. If they nod at any of these, they're already half-convinced.
Problem Cards (Visitor-Centric)
Card 1: "Your Website Is Slow"
plain
[Icon: Clock/Turtle]

**"Our website takes forever to load."

You know it. Your customers feel it. Slow websites kill conversions 
— 53% of mobile users abandon a site that takes more than 3 seconds. 
Google also penalizes slow sites in search rankings.

If your competitors' sites load faster, they're winning your customers 
while you're still loading.
Why this works: Uses statistics (credibility). Creates urgency (competitors winning). No jargon.
Card 2: "It Doesn't Work on Mobile"
plain
[Icon: Broken Phone]

**"Our site looks terrible on phones."

Over 60% of your customers are browsing on mobile. If your website 
isn't designed for thumb-scrolling, pinch-zooming, and small screens, 
you're losing more than half your potential business.

A mobile-unfriendly site doesn't just look bad — it tells customers 
you don't care about their experience.
Why this works: Quantifies the loss (60%). Taps into the emotional consequence ("you don't care").
Card 3: "No One Can Find Us Online"
plain
[Icon: Search with X]

**"We don't show up on Google."

You built a website, but it's invisible. Without proper SEO structure, 
semantic markup, and performance optimization, search engines can't 
understand — or rank — your site.

Being on page 2 of Google is like being invisible. Your customers 
are searching, but they're finding your competitors instead.
Why this works: "Invisible" is a powerful metaphor. Connects technical SEO to business outcome (competitors winning).
Card 4: "Visitors Don't Take Action"
plain
[Icon: Confused User]

**"People visit but don't call or buy."

Traffic without conversion is just vanity. If visitors can't figure 
out what to do next — or if your contact form is buried three clicks 
deep — they'll leave and never return.

Your website should guide visitors naturally toward the next step: 
calling you, booking a service, or making a purchase.
Why this works: Names a specific UX failure (buried contact form). Offers the ideal outcome.
Card 5: "It Looks Outdated"
plain
[Icon: Old Computer]

**"Our site looks like it's from 2015."

First impressions matter. An outdated website makes your business 
look unprofessional, untrustworthy, or even closed. In a world where 
customers judge credibility in 0.05 seconds, your design is your handshake.

Your website should reflect the quality of your work — not undermine it.
Why this works: The 0.05-second stat is shocking. "Handshake" metaphor makes it relatable.
Card 6: "We Can't Update It Ourselves"
plain
[Icon: Locked Code]

**"We need a developer for every small change."

Every text update, every new photo, every price change requires 
calling a developer and waiting days (and paying more). Your website 
should empower you, not make you dependent.

I build sites that are easy to maintain — or I document everything 
so clearly that you (or your team) can make updates confidently.
Why this works: Addresses the hidden cost of websites (ongoing dependency). Offers independence.
